1. Finance / Earnings Section
   The Finance or Earnings section should be expanded to provide vendors with a clearer breakdown of their financial activity.
   Required additions:
   Total earnings
   Tax breakdown
   Platform commission
   Vendor payout summary
   Order-level earnings breakdown
   Date-based filtering for earnings
   The vendor should be able to understand gross sales, deductions, commissions, taxes, and net payout from one dashboard.
2. Settings Section (Vendor Portal)
   The Settings area should be reorganized to include the following subsections:
   Merchant account details
   Password management
   User information
   Business details
   Email address
   Payment/payout information
   Notification preferences
   This section should allow vendors to manage their profile, account access, business information, and payout-related details.
3. Vendor Support / “Message Us”
   Add a Message Us or Contact Support feature within the vendor portal.
   Purpose:
   Allow vendors to contact OrderLynk support directly from the vendor area.
   Support requests may include payment issues, order issues, product upload support, account questions, or technical problems.
   This can initially be implemented as a simple contact form or support message module.
4. Vendor FAQs
   Add a dedicated FAQs section within the vendor portal.
   Suggested FAQ topics:
   How to create products
   How to manage orders
   How payment and commission work
   How to update fulfillment status
   How to message customers
   How to upload logo/banner
   How to set low-stock alerts
   How to contact support
   The goal is to reduce vendor dependency on manual support.
5. Vendor Users and Staff Access
   Vendors should be able to manage account users.
   Required user types:
   Owner / Primary contact
   Sub-contact / Staff user
   Staff users should be able to help manage orders, products, fulfillment, and customer communication based on permissions.
   Suggested roles:
   Owner
   Manager
   Order Fulfillment Staff
   Customer Support Staff
   Access control should be considered so not every staff member has access to finance or account settings.
6. Low Stock Alert
   Vendors should be able to set low-stock alerts for products.
   Required functionality:
   Vendor sets a low-stock threshold per product.
   System alerts vendor when stock falls below the threshold.
   Low-stock products should be visible on the dashboard or product management page.
   Example:
   If the vendor sets the alert threshold for Garri 5kg to 10 units, the system should notify the vendor when quantity reaches 10 or below.
